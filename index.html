I am an M.S. Scholar at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, where I work on the application of graph neural networks to wireless communication systems. My research focuses on channel state information (CSI) compression and estimation, with an emphasis on learning efficient representations for high-dimensional wireless channels. I am advised by Prof. Lakshmi Narasimhan Theagarajan.

Prior to joining IIT Madras, I spent seven months at the Indian Institute of Science as part of the Network Engineering Lab, where I worked on data plane development for ADWISER, focusing on high-performance packet processing and system-level optimizations.

I graduated in 2024 with a Bachelor’s degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from PES University, Bangalore. For research collaborations, feel free to reach out to me via email.
